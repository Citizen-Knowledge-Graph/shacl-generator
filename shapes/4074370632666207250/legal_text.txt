1. You can receive child benefits for your children if they reside and are registered in Germany, another EU country, Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, or Switzerland, regardless of their nationality.

2. You can receive child benefits for your biological children, stepchildren, adopted children, grandchildren living with you, and foster children under certain conditions.

3. You cannot receive child benefits if you or your children receive comparable benefits from an international or supranational institution.

4. You cannot receive child benefits if you or your children receive comparable benefits from abroad, even if these are lower than the German child benefits.

5. If you receive family benefits from an EU member state, Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, or Switzerland, you may be eligible for a differential amount as partial child benefits.

6. You can receive child benefits until your child turns 18 years old.

7. You can receive child benefits until your child turns 21 if they are unemployed and registered as a job seeker.

8. You can receive child benefits until your child turns 25 if they are in education, in a transition period of up to four months, unable to find an apprenticeship, or engaged in recognized voluntary service.

9. You are eligible for child benefits for each month in which you meet all the requirements for at least one day.

10. You must inform the family benefits office immediately of any changes that may affect your child benefits, such as employment changes, relocation, or changes in family status.

11. You are legally obligated to report changes that affect your child benefits, and failure to do so may constitute an administrative offense or a criminal act.

12. The family benefits office will periodically review your eligibility for child benefits and may require you to complete a questionnaire and provide additional documents.

13. If you disagree with a decision by the family benefits office, you can file an appeal within one month of receiving the decision, either in writing or in person.

14. If your appeal is denied, you can file a lawsuit with the financial court within one month of receiving the denial.

15. If you received child benefits without meeting all the requirements, you must repay the excess amount received.

16. If you disagree with a repayment demand, you can appeal, but you must still repay the amount while the appeal is pending.

17. In a household with both parents, you can decide which parent receives the child benefits, even if one is not the biological parent, provided you are married or in a registered partnership.

18. For separated parents, the parent with whom the child primarily resides receives the child benefits.

19. If the child does not live with either parent, the parent providing the higher support receives the child benefits, or the parents can mutually decide who receives it if support is equal.
